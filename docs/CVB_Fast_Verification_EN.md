üî∑ **Questions for Self-Testing Religious Systems**  
 These questions are intended for voluntary evaluation by any system or individual,  
 without judgment and with full respect for freedom.

1. **Motivation: Why does a person believe?**  
    Is the goal a voluntary choice of **Truth and Good** ‚Äî or a desire for personal reward, status, or safety?  
    Is selfless sacrifice possible without harm to others ‚Äî even without a promised reward?  
    Does motivation endure without the expectation of gain?  
    Are **Good and Evil** distinguished by logical consistency and motivation ‚Äî or by decree of Authority?

2. **Image of the Source: What is the nature of the ‚ÄúGod‚Äù presented?**  
    Is it a Ruler acting through fear, demanding submission?  
    Or a Person who respects freedom and sacrifices for others?  
    Is the Source capable of voluntary sacrifice and participation?  
    Does It allow internal contradictions for the sake of outcomes?

3. **Ethics and Organization: How does the system act?**  
    Is respect and distinction encouraged ‚Äî or is fear and coercion imposed?  
    **Financial transparency:**  
    ‚Äì Are donations entirely voluntary?  
    ‚Äì Are sources and expenditures publicly accessible?

üìä **Common Logical-Ontological Paradoxes**  
 (Observed in the majority of religious systems during self-testing)

‚ùå **1\. Substitution of the Source**  
 The system claims one Source of Truth, but in practice replaces or denies it ‚Äî creating a contradiction.

‚ùå **2\. Loss of Distinguishability**  
 Good and Evil, Personhood and role, Truth and Falsehood lose their distinction ‚Äî making it impossible to know what is what.

‚ùå **3\. Blockage of Freedom**  
 Genuine freedom of choice is not granted ‚Äî choice is removed by predestination or inaccessible mystery.

‚ùå **4\. Violation of Causality**  
 The system breaks the connection between choice and result ‚Äî promising salvation, forgiveness, or reward without conscious choice, effort, or transformation, rendering freedom and responsibility meaningless.

‚ùå **5\. Self-Excluding System**  
 The system claims what logically cancels itself ‚Äî for example, recognizing a source it also deems corrupted, or demanding the impossible.

‚úÖ **Indicators of Consistency**  
 (Observed in rare systems that align with the CVB model)

‚úÖ **1\. No Substitution of the Source**  
 The Source of Truth is acknowledged as one, internally consistent, and never replaced.

‚úÖ **2\. No Loss of Distinguishability**  
 Good, Personhood, freedom, and Truth remain clearly distinguishable and are not merged with falsehood or external domination.

‚úÖ **3\. No Blockage of Freedom**  
 Freedom of choice is fully preserved: the choice is neither predetermined nor inaccessible to understanding.

‚úÖ **4\. No Violation of Causality**  
 Causal links between motivation, choice, and consequence are logically consistent.

‚úÖ **5\. No Self-Exclusion**  
 The system does not contradict itself, does not make logically impossible claims, and does not destroy its own foundation.
